Study 6

Study 7

Demonstrates reliable BroadcastChannel communication between sender and receiver, based on Promises in the sender.

Before the sender adds a new item to the list, it makes sure that the receiver window is open and listens to messages from the sender. This is different to study 8.

To persist the items in the list, the receiver will have to use local storage. This is explored in study 8.

Study 8

Demonstrates saving a list of items in local storage in the receiver. Every time the receiver is opened or reloaded, the list of items is restored from local storage. If the sender appends a new item to the list, the receiver is notified via storage events, and can then update the list of items in its window, thereby eliminating the need for a communication channel between sender and receiver.

The sender even can successfully append new items when the receiver window is closed, they will appear the next time the receiver window is opened.
